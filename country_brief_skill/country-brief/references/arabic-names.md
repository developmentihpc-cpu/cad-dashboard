# Arabic Proper-Name Register

Standardized Arabic forms for leaders, places, institutions, and key proper nouns appearing in country briefs. **Use the form listed here consistently across all Arabic briefs.** Add new names to the appropriate section as they're encountered.

When the source material uses multiple Arabic forms for the same name (e.g., Bainimarama appearing as باينيماراما and بايني ماراما), pick the form most widely used in Arabic-language Reuters / Al Jazeera / Al Sharq Al Awsat coverage and standardize on it.

## Conventions notes

**Transliteration principle.** The brief uses Arabic-press standard forms, prioritizing what appears in Al Jazeera / Reuters Arabic / Al Sharq Al Awsat coverage. When two forms compete, the form more widely used in academic/professional writing wins.

**Honorifics.** Use الشيخ for Gulf rulers and senior figures by convention; سماحة or آية الله for senior Shia clerics where the source materials use such forms; otherwise no honorifics in body text.

**First-name + last-name patterns.** Use full name on first reference; last name only after that. The English source "Pezeshkian said" becomes "بزشكيان" in subsequent references after first introduction of مسعود بزشكيان.

**Transliteration of Persian names.** Persian names in Arabic press are typically rendered with their Persian-letter equivalents normalized to Arabic letters: پ → ب, چ → ج (some sources keep پ, چ; we use the Arabic-letter forms for consistency unless the Persian-letter form is unambiguously standard).

**Patronymic/title compounds.** Iranian and some other names sometimes include titles or patronymics. Render the title in Arabic (الإمام الخميني, آية الله السيستاني) when it's part of standard usage; omit when it's optional or formal-only.
