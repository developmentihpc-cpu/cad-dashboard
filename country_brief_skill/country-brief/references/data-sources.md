# Authoritative Data Sources for Country Briefs

This catalog organizes trusted sources by domain. The principle: when there's an authoritative institutional source for a claim, use it; never lean on news aggregators or general web pages when primary data is available. Authoritative sources also give the brief credibility — readers know what World Bank WDI is; they don't know what randomwebsite.com is.

## Source quality hierarchy (apply throughout)

1. **Authoritative multilateral data** — IMF, World Bank, UN, OECD, regional development banks
2. **Recognized indices / monitors** — V-Dem, ACLED, Freedom House, Transparency International, INFORM
3. **Primary national sources** — central banks, statistics offices, electoral commissions, official gazettes
4. **Specialized think tanks** — Crisis Group, Brookings, Chatham House, CFR, IISS, regional institutes
5. **Quality journalism** — Reuters, AP, FT, BBC, Economist, regional quality outlets
6. **Aggregators and secondary sources** — last resort, and only when primary sources are unavailable

## Domain-by-domain catalog

### Macroeconomic & fiscal

- **IMF World Economic Outlook (WEO)** — biannual baseline projections. https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WEO
- **IMF Article IV consultations** — detailed annual country reviews; the single most authoritative macro document.
- **IMF Country Reports** — companion reports to Article IV, often with sectoral deep-dives.
- **World Bank World Development Indicators (WDI)** — 1,400+ indicators, time series. https://databank.worldbank.org/source/world-development-indicators
- **World Bank Country Economic Memorandum (CEM)** — produced every 3–5 years per country, deep structural analysis.
- **World Bank Macro Poverty Outlook (MPO)** — twice-yearly country-level macro updates.
- **IMF DataMapper** — quick comparable data. https://www.imf.org/external/datamapper/
- **National central banks** — for current monetary policy stance, FX, reserves.
- **National statistics offices** — for headline GDP, inflation, employment (verify against multilateral sources, since methodologies vary).

### Sovereign debt & rating

- **IMF Debt Sustainability Analysis (DSA)** — embedded in Article IV.
- **World Bank International Debt Statistics** — quarterly debt data.
- **S&P / Moody's / Fitch** sovereign rating reports — paywalled but credit summaries are public.
- **Bloomberg / Refinitiv** for spread data if available.

### Governance & institutional quality

- **Worldwide Governance Indicators (WGI)** — six dimensions (Voice & Accountability, Political Stability, Government Effectiveness, Regulatory Quality, Rule of Law, Control of Corruption), 200+ countries, 1996–present, updated annually. https://www.worldbank.org/en/publication/worldwide-governance-indicators
- **V-Dem (Varieties of Democracy)** — granular democracy and governance data. https://v-dem.net/
- **Bertelsmann Transformation Index (BTI)** — biennial assessments of political and economic transformation in 137 developing/transitioning countries. https://bti-project.org/
- **Freedom House** — annual "Freedom in the World" + "Freedom on the Net" + "Nations in Transit." https://freedomhouse.org/
- **CPIA (Country Policy and Institutional Assessment)** — World Bank rating of 16 criteria across four clusters; available for IDA-eligible countries.
- **Open Budget Survey (IBP)** — fiscal transparency.

### Corruption

- **Transparency International Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI)** — annual ranking. https://www.transparency.org/en/cpi
- **Control of Corruption (WGI dimension)** — alternative composite.
- **Basel AML Index** — money laundering and terrorist financing risk.
- **TRACE Bribery Risk Matrix** — business bribery risk.

### State fragility & conflict risk

- **Fragile States Index (Fund for Peace)** — annual, 178 countries, 12 indicators across 4 clusters. https://fragilestatesindex.org/
- **OECD States of Fragility** — biennial report, OECD framework.
- **CPIA Cluster D** (public sector management and institutions) — World Bank fragility lens.
- **World Bank Harmonized List of Fragile and Conflict-Affected Situations** — annual official classification.
- **INFORM Risk Index (JRC / European Commission)** — 191 countries on humanitarian crisis and disaster risk. https://drmkc.jrc.ec.europa.eu/inform-index

### Conflict & security

- **ACLED (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data)** — geolocated, near-real-time conflict event data. https://acleddata.com/
- **UCDP (Uppsala Conflict Data Program)** — long-running conflict dataset. https://ucdp.uu.se/
- **International Crisis Group (ICG)** — country reports, CrisisWatch monthly. https://www.crisisgroup.org/
- **CrisisWatch** — monthly alert tracker.
- **IISS Armed Conflict Survey** — annual.
- **SIPRI** — military expenditure, arms transfers.
- **GTI (Global Terrorism Index)** — annual ranking.
- **ICRC** — situation updates in active conflict zones.

### Humanitarian

- **INFORM Risk Index** — see fragility above.
- **OCHA Humanitarian Needs Overview (HNO)** — country-level needs and PIN. https://www.unocha.org/
- **OCHA Humanitarian Response Plan (HRP)** — annual response plans with funding tracking.
- **IPC (Integrated Food Security Phase Classification)** — food security severity (Phases 1–5, with Phase 5 = famine). https://www.ipcinfo.org/
- **FEWS NET** — early warning for food insecurity. https://fews.net/
- **UNHCR Refugee Data Finder** — refugee, IDP, stateless data. https://www.unhcr.org/refugee-statistics/
- **IOM Displacement Tracking Matrix (DTM)** — IDP tracking in priority countries.
- **WHO** — health emergencies.

### Human development & social

- **UNDP Human Development Report (HDR) & HDI** — annual. https://hdr.undp.org/
- **World Bank Poverty and Inequality Platform** — poverty rates at multiple lines.
- **WHO Global Health Observatory** — health indicators.
- **UNESCO Institute for Statistics** — education.
- **UN Women / OECD Gender Index** — gender-disaggregated data.
- **ILO** — labor markets.

### Trade, investment, business environment

- **WTO trade statistics** — bilateral and multilateral.
- **UN Comtrade** — granular trade data.
- **UNCTAD World Investment Report** — FDI trends.
- **B-READY (World Bank)** — successor to Doing Business, business-environment indicators.
- **WEF Global Competitiveness Report** — historical reference; check current edition status.
- **MIGA / political risk providers** — political risk insurance and assessment.
- **OECD Investment Policy Reviews** — for OECD-engaged countries.

### Sanctions

- **OFAC (US Treasury)** — SDN list, sectoral sanctions, FAQs. https://ofac.treasury.gov/
- **EU Council sanctions** — consolidated list, sectoral measures. https://www.sanctionsmap.eu/
- **UN Security Council** — Consolidated List, sanctions committees. https://www.un.org/securitycouncil/sanctions/information
- **UK OFSI** — UK sanctions list.
- **OpenSanctions.org** — aggregator of public lists (good first-pass tool).

### Climate & environment

- **ND-GAIN (Notre Dame Global Adaptation Initiative)** — vulnerability and readiness index. https://gain.nd.edu/
- **World Bank Climate Change Knowledge Portal (CCKP)** — country climate data. https://climateknowledgeportal.worldbank.org/
- **IPCC** — global assessment reports with regional chapters.
- **NDCs (Nationally Determined Contributions)** — official emissions pledges. https://unfccc.int/NDCREG
- **Climate Action Tracker** — assessment of country climate ambition.
- **EDGAR / Climate Watch** — emissions data.

### Civil society & media

- **CIVICUS Monitor** — civic space ratings. https://monitor.civicus.org/
- **Reporters Without Borders Press Freedom Index** — annual. https://rsf.org/en/index
- **Article 19** — freedom of expression assessments.
- **Freedom on the Net (Freedom House)** — internet freedom.
- **Access Now / NetBlocks** — internet shutdown tracking.

### Elections

- **National electoral commissions** — official results and calendar.
- **EU Election Observation Missions** — published reports.
- **Carter Center** — election observation.
- **OSCE/ODIHR** — election observation reports for OSCE region.
- **NDI / IFES** — election assistance and observation.
- **IDEA International** — election design and integrity data.
- **V-Dem electoral integrity indicators**.

### Development assistance & aid

- **OECD DAC (Development Assistance Committee)** — official donor data. https://www.oecd.org/dac/
- **OECD CRS (Creditor Reporting System)** — project-level aid data.
- **IATI (International Aid Transparency Initiative)** — aid activity data.
- **AidData** — Chinese development finance, others.

### Country-specific deep sources

- **U.S. State Department Country Reports** — Human Rights, Religious Freedom, Trafficking in Persons, Investment Climate Statements; useful, though note their official-US-government perspective.
- **U.S. CIA World Factbook** — quick reference baseline.
- **UK FCDO travel and country information** — official UK perspective.
- **EU External Action Service country pages**.
- **Regional development bank country strategies** — AfDB, ADB, IDB, EBRD.

## Sources to be cautious with

- **Wikipedia** — useful as a starting orientation, but never cite as a source for a country brief. Trace claims to underlying sources.
- **News aggregators** (Yahoo News, etc.) — go to the original outlet.
- **Country-promotion sites** (investment promotion agencies, tourism boards) — useful for understanding the government's pitch, but flag as such; don't treat as independent data.
- **Single-country advocacy outlets** — useful for understanding particular perspectives, but balance.
- **Older than 3 years** — flag explicitly. The most common failure mode is leaning on a 2019 statistic in a 2026 brief.

## Picking sources by question

Common analyst questions and the strongest source for each:

- **"What's the current GDP growth?"** → IMF WEO (most recent vintage) + national central bank
- **"How stable is the political system?"** → V-Dem + Fragile States Index + ICG country page
- **"What's the corruption picture?"** → CPI + WGI Control of Corruption + qualitative (TI national chapter)
- **"What's the current conflict situation?"** → ACLED (last 30/90/365 days) + ICG CrisisWatch (most recent month)
- **"What's the humanitarian situation?"** → OCHA HNO + IPC + UNHCR
- **"Is the country under sanctions?"** → OFAC + EU Sanctions Map + UN Security Council Consolidated List
- **"What's the business environment like?"** → B-READY + recent FDI flows (UNCTAD) + sectoral chambers
- **"What's the human development picture?"** → HDI + World Bank poverty data + sectoral UN agencies
- **"What's the climate exposure?"** → ND-GAIN + World Bank CCKP + national NDC

## Triangulation rule

For any major analytic judgment, use at least two independent sources from different categories. Three is better. If only one source supports a claim, treat the claim as preliminary and say so.
