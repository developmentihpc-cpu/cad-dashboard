# Section Library: Optional Modules for Country Briefs

This file is the menu of optional sections to insert at section 7 of the country brief spine. Pick modules based on the user's stated purpose. Do not include every module — irrelevant sections make the brief feel padded.

## How to use this menu

- Read the user's stated purpose and angle.
- Pick 1–4 modules that directly serve that purpose.
- Don't include a module just because the country has that issue; include it only if the brief's reader needs it for their decision.
- If unsure between two modules, pick the more decision-relevant one.

---

## Module: Development Project Environment

**Use when:** The user is evaluating a development project, programming, or country portfolio.
**Length:** 1–1.5 pages
**Anchored on:** OECD DAC data, World Bank CPIA, partner-country priorities documents.

### Subsections

1. **Donor landscape and aid architecture**
   - Top 5 donors by volume (DAC data), trends over 5 years
   - Sector concentration of aid (where donors are clustered, where there are gaps)
   - Coordination mechanisms (donor working groups, joint strategies)
2. **Government absorptive capacity**
   - CPIA score and trajectory
   - Public financial management quality (PEFA assessments if available)
   - Implementation history of similar projects (cite specific examples)
3. **Sectoral readiness for the proposed intervention**
   - Existing institutions, capacity, and ongoing reforms
   - Recent sector-specific reviews (joint sector reviews, PER, etc.)
4. **Operating environment for implementers**
   - NGO registration regime, civil society space, visa and access
   - Currency and transfer constraints if any
5. **Lessons from prior interventions**
   - 2–3 specific previous projects with outcomes and what they taught

## Module: Humanitarian Severity

**Use when:** The user is assessing humanitarian needs, crisis response, or operating in a fragile/conflict context.
**Length:** 1.5–2 pages
**Anchored on:** IPC, OCHA Humanitarian Needs Overview, UNHCR, INFORM, ACLED.

### Subsections

1. **Severity overview**
   - INFORM Risk index score and rank
   - IPC food security classification by sub-national area
   - Number of people in need (PIN) per OCHA HNO
2. **Displacement**
   - IDPs and refugees: numbers, locations, trajectories
   - Cross-border dynamics if applicable
3. **Conflict dynamics affecting humanitarian access**
   - Active conflict zones (cite ACLED)
   - Bureaucratic and security access constraints
4. **Humanitarian response architecture**
   - Cluster system status, lead agencies
   - HRP funding level and gap
5. **Protection environment**
   - Civilian harm trends
   - Gender-based violence indicators if available
6. **Scenario outlook (6–12 months)**
   - Most likely trajectory, deterioration scenario, improvement scenario

## Module: Market Entry / Business Environment

**Use when:** The user is evaluating a country for trade, investment, or market expansion.
**Length:** 1.5–2 pages
**Anchored on:** B-READY (World Bank successor to Doing Business), WEF Global Competitiveness, sector-specific market data.

### Subsections

1. **Market size and structure**
   - Population, urbanization, consumer purchasing power
   - Relevant sector size and growth (cite trade data, sector studies)
2. **Entry pathways**
   - Foreign investment regime, sectoral restrictions, ownership rules
   - Tax regime, repatriation of profits, treaty network
3. **Operating costs**
   - Labor (cost, skills availability), real estate, utilities, logistics
   - Regulatory burden (B-READY readiness measures)
4. **Competitive landscape**
   - Incumbent players (domestic and foreign)
   - Customer behavior and channel realities
5. **Distribution and logistics**
   - Trade infrastructure, port and customs efficiency
   - Domestic distribution networks
6. **Currency and payment risk**
   - FX regime, convertibility, payment infrastructure

## Module: Sanctions Exposure

**Use when:** The user is assessing legal or reputational risk linked to a sanctioned regime or sanctioned actors.
**Length:** 1–1.5 pages
**Anchored on:** OFAC SDN list, EU consolidated list, UN Security Council sanctions list, official sanctions guidance.

### Subsections

1. **Active sanctions regimes**
   - US (OFAC), EU, UN, UK, other jurisdictions as relevant
   - Comprehensive vs. targeted (sectoral, list-based)
2. **Listed entities and individuals**
   - Headline listings (count, recent additions)
   - Sectoral implications (financial, energy, defense)
3. **Secondary sanctions risk**
   - Where third parties have been penalized for dealing with listed parties
4. **Licensing and exemptions**
   - General licenses (humanitarian, agricultural, etc.)
   - Specific license pathways
5. **Enforcement trajectory**
   - Recent enforcement actions and the signal they send
6. **Compliance implications**
   - What due diligence is required for the user's contemplated activity

⚠️ This module touches on legal exposure. Always note that the brief is informational, not legal advice, and that sanctions guidance from qualified counsel and current official sources (OFAC FAQs, EU Council notices) should be consulted for specific transactions.

## Module: Regulatory Regime

**Use when:** The user needs to understand the regulatory environment for a sector or activity.
**Length:** 1–1.5 pages
**Anchored on:** Sector regulators' official sites, primary legislation, peer-reviewed legal analyses.

### Subsections

1. **Legal and regulatory framework**
   - Primary legislation, key regulators, supervisory architecture
2. **Recent regulatory changes**
   - Major reforms in the last 24–36 months
   - Direction of travel (liberalizing, tightening)
3. **Compliance regime**
   - Licensing, reporting, audit, anti-money laundering requirements
4. **Enforcement practice**
   - Capacity and consistency of enforcement (gap between law and practice)
5. **Pending reforms**
   - Legislation under consideration, likely effective dates

## Module: Climate Vulnerability

**Use when:** The user is planning long-horizon investments, infrastructure, or operations in climate-exposed sectors.
**Length:** 1 page
**Anchored on:** ND-GAIN, World Bank Climate Change Knowledge Portal, IPCC regional chapters, NDCs.

### Subsections

1. **Physical climate exposure**
   - Temperature and precipitation trends, projections to 2050
   - Hazard exposure (drought, flood, cyclone, sea-level rise)
   - ND-GAIN exposure and vulnerability scores
2. **Sectoral implications**
   - Agriculture, water, energy, infrastructure as relevant
3. **Adaptation readiness**
   - National Adaptation Plan status, financing flows
4. **Transition exposure**
   - Fossil-fuel dependence, stranded asset risk
   - NDC ambition and credibility

## Module: Election Cycle / Political Transition

**Use when:** The country has an election within 18 months, or is undergoing a leadership transition.
**Length:** ¾–1 page
**Anchored on:** National electoral commission, international observer reports (EU EOM, Carter Center, OSCE/ODIHR), V-Dem electoral integrity data.

### Subsections

1. **Electoral calendar and stakes**
   - Date, type (presidential, legislative, local), key offices
2. **Field of contenders**
   - Ruling party / incumbent: strengths, vulnerabilities
   - Opposition: realistic competitiveness, fragmentation
3. **Electoral integrity environment**
   - Voter registration, media freedom, observer access
   - Historical baseline (past elections, observer judgments)
4. **Likely outcomes and second-order effects**
   - Most plausible scenarios, what each means for the user's decision

## Module: Project / Operating Environment (Detailed)

**Use when:** The user is making a go/no-go decision on a specific operation or project in-country.
**Length:** 1.5–2 pages
**Anchored on:** MIGA political risk, internal organization experience, peer-organization reports.

### Subsections

1. **Project-relevant political alignment**
   - Government priorities and how the project maps
2. **Counterparty risk**
   - Government partner capacity, reputation, prior project history
3. **Reputational risk**
   - Potential association risk (e.g., human rights, environmental, governance issues)
4. **Operational risk**
   - Permitting, land, labor, security
   - Currency, transfer, banking
5. **Exit pathways**
   - What does a managed exit look like if things deteriorate
6. **Recommended risk-management actions**
   - Specific mitigations the user should consider

## Module: Macro Stress / Debt Sustainability Deep-Dive

**Use when:** The user is concerned about sovereign-credit, currency, or banking stress.
**Length:** 1 page
**Anchored on:** IMF Article IV and DSA, World Bank Debt Sustainability Framework, central bank publications, rating agencies.

### Subsections

1. **Debt trajectory**
   - Total public debt / GDP, composition (external/domestic, FX/local)
   - Debt service profile, near-term maturities
2. **IMF assessment**
   - Latest Article IV bottom line, DSA risk rating
   - Program status (under program, off-track, no program)
3. **Currency and reserves**
   - FX regime, reserve adequacy
   - Capital account openness
4. **Banking sector stability**
   - NPL ratios, capital adequacy
   - Sovereign-bank loop exposure
5. **Stress scenarios**
   - Plausible stress paths and policy responses

## Module: Civil Society & Media Environment

**Use when:** The user is operating with or through civil society, or where media freedom is material to the decision.
**Length:** ¾ page
**Anchored on:** CIVICUS Monitor, Reporters Without Borders Press Freedom Index, Freedom House, Article 19.

### Subsections

1. **Civil society space**
   - CIVICUS Monitor rating and trend
   - NGO registration and operating environment
2. **Media freedom**
   - RSF Press Freedom Index score and rank
   - State of independent media, harassment patterns
3. **Digital rights**
   - Internet freedom (Freedom on the Net), shutdown history
   - Surveillance regime
4. **Operating implications**
   - What this means for transparency, communications, advocacy

---

## Module: Diaspora & Remittances

**Use when:** Diaspora flows are material to the country's economy or to the user's planned activity.
**Length:** ½ page
**Anchored on:** World Bank Migration and Remittances data, IOM.

Subsections cover remittance volumes, top corridors, formal vs. informal channels, recent trends, and policy environment.

## How to combine modules

Common combinations:

- **Development project brief**: Project Environment + (Humanitarian Severity if fragile) + Election Cycle (if relevant)
- **Investment / market entry brief**: Market Entry + Sanctions + Climate (if long-horizon) + Macro Stress (if EM)
- **Crisis / humanitarian brief**: Humanitarian Severity + Operating Environment + Civil Society
- **Political risk brief**: Election Cycle + Civil Society + Sanctions + Macro Stress
- **Due diligence brief**: Sanctions + Regulatory Regime + Operating Environment + Civil Society

If a combination would push the brief past 20 pages, prioritize the modules most relevant to the immediate decision and move secondary material to a short appendix.
